Installation

We Provide installation services for all types of switchyard equipment’s. We have a team of professionals who are expert in the field of installation of switchyard Equipment’s ( Power transformer, Switchgear protection relay, Instruments Transformer and Isolators.) Here's as an explanation of the various aspects involved in the installation.

  • Site Preparation: This phase involves surveying the site, determining suitable locations for the substation components, and ensuring compliance with safety and environmental regulations.
  • Foundation Construction: Foundations for equipment such as transformers, circuit breakers, and switchgear are constructed to provide stable support and grounding.
  • Equipment Installation: Various components including transformers, circuit breakers, switchgear, control panels, and associated wiring are installed according to engineering drawings and specifications.
  • Cable Laying: Underground cables or overhead lines are laid to connect the substation to the power grid and other relevant infrastructure.

Maintenance

Maintenance the switchyard Equipment’s is very important to ensure the safety and reliability of all equipment’s. We have a team of professionals who are expert in the field of maintenance. All the switchyard equipment’s including control and relay panel, Circuit Breaker, Isolators, etc. Here's as an explanation of the various aspects involved in the Maintenance.

  • Regular Inspections: Scheduled inspections are conducted to identify any signs of wear, damage, or malfunction in equipment and infrastructure.
  • Preventive Maintenance: Routine maintenance tasks such as cleaning, lubrication, and tightening of connections are performed to prevent potential failures and ensure optimal performance.
  • Equipment Testing: Periodic testing of equipment functionality, insulation resistance, and protective relays is carried out to verify operational integrity.
  • Repair and Replacement: Any faulty or worn-out components are repaired or replaced promptly to minimize downtime and maintain system reliability.

Commissioning

Commissioning is the systematic process by which a piece of equipment, system, or facility is tested to verify that it functions in accordance with the design intent and owners operational requirements. Our years of experience in commissioning and testing of equipment’s and system will make all your system and equipment’s safe and reliable. Here's as an explanation of the various aspects involved in the Commissioning.

  • Functional Testing: Each component and system within the substation is tested to ensure proper operation according to design specifications.
  • Integration Testing: Integration tests are conducted to verify the interoperability and coordination between different components and subsystems.
  • Performance Evaluation: The performance of the substation under various load conditions and contingencies is evaluated to confirm compliance with performance standards and regulatory requirements.
  • Documentation: Comprehensive documentation including as-built drawings, equipment manuals, test reports, and commissioning records is compiled to provide a record of the commissioning process and facilitate future maintenance and troubleshooting.

Testing

Testing is the only way to identify manufacturing fault that could compromise the electrical safety of a product out in the field. We are expertise in AIS/ GIS switchyards equipment’s testing. Here's as an explanation of the various aspects involved in the Testing.

  • Primary Injection Testing: This involves injecting a current directly into the primary side of protective devices to verify their operation and calibration.
  • Secondary Injection Testing: Secondary injection tests are conducted on protective relays and associated control circuits to ensure proper coordination and response to fault conditions.
  • Transformer Testing: Transformer testing includes insulation resistance tests, turns ratio tests, and magnetic balance tests to assess the condition and performance of the transformer.
  • Circuit Breaker Testing: Various tests such as timing tests, contact resistance tests, and insulation resistance tests are performed to verify the reliability and functionality of circuit breakers.
